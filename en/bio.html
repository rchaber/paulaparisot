Bio

Paula Parisot was born in Rio de Janeiro and currently lives in São Paulo. After earning a degree in Industrial Design, she was awarded a scholarship to continue her studies at the New School University in New York, where she received a M.F.A. Her first book of short stories The Lady of solitude (A dama da solidão, Companhia das letras, 2007) has been translated to Spanish, and some of the stories appeared publications in different countries as United States, Colombia, Mexico. She is also the author of the novel Hinges and screws (Gonzos e parafusos, Leya, 2010), which inspired a performance that lasted 7 days and 6 days. Her new novel To leave (Partir) is due to be released this year in Brazil and Mexico.
