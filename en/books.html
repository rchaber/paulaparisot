The Lady of Solitude

Read sample

In The Lady of solitude, Paula Parisot's characters are women facing ancestor dilemas in our days and the protagonists face them under the prism of all behavior changes occurred in the last decades. But that doesn't mean they are all strong, decided, emancipated: they are just inhabiting a world where the relationships - love, family and friendship - changed.

For instance, there is a girl that, searching for social ascension, finds out the true nature of her enchanted prince. There is also the woman that, motivated by curiosity, joins an erotic theater group which main work is to reenact flesh and bones versions of classical paintings, tableau vivent. Like her, the other protagonists of this book give voice to a world of inquietations that the author skillfully translated in an audacious prose. Alternating the short stories narrative approach between inner monologue, letters and the oniscient, Parisot brings to the surface the intimate movements that conduct our behaviour, as well as the precise moment when the rules change, advance or simply fall to oblivion.

Hinges and Screws

Read sample

A woman can be painted in different ways. The same painting can be viewed from different angles. Isabela is like a woman painted by Klimt. Or is it Schiele? At the same time beautiful and grotesque. A young psychoanalist on the verge of schizofrenia, messing up her patients with literature characters, trying to find the frontiers of her own being and dragging herself through a fragile routine in which each breadth can be suffocating. Isabela lives a love triangle with no love. Treats madness as life's side effect. Reviews her own past with a suicidal denial, trying to avoid being condemned to the everyday's tragedy.
